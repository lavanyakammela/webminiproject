When did the Breakfast appear in the city?

The word "tiffin" comes from the English word "tiffing", which means to have a small drink. The term "tiffin" was used in the British Raj to refer to afternoon tea, but it came to mean lunch for Anglo-Indians in northern British India by 1867. The term "tiffin" is used in India to refer to a light lunch, a snack, tea time, and a packed lunch. It was from 1864.(Source:Detailed about the tiffin)
